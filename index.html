Emergency Ebola Prevention and Community Protection Response

North Kivu, Democratic Republic of the Congo

Urgent Action to Protect Communities from Ebola in North Kivu

Communities across Nyiragongo, Rutshuru and Masisi are facing the combined impacts of conflict, displacement, poverty, and the resurgence of Ebola Virus Disease (EVD).

Background

North Kivu Province continues to experience armed conflict, mass displacement, food insecurity, and limited access to healthcare. The resurgence of Ebola has further increased the vulnerability of women, children, displaced families and people living in remote areas.

Misinformation, fear, stigma and limited access to reliable health information continue to undermine prevention efforts.

Who We Are

UPDCI asbl (Union de Peuples pour le Développement Communautaire Intègre) is a community-based and feminist civil society organization founded in 2022 and based in Nyiragongo Territory, North Kivu.

Our team has more than 13 years of grassroots experience in humanitarian action, protection, prevention of gender-based violence, PSEA, human rights documentation, youth empowerment and community awareness.

Why This Emergency Matters

  • Increased mortality among vulnerable populations
  • Fear and misinformation within communities
  • Stigmatization of survivors and affected families
  • Increased vulnerability of women and girls
  • Disruption of education and livelihoods
  • Additional pressure on fragile health systems

What We Are Doing

1. Community Awareness and Education

  • Door-to-door awareness campaigns
  • Community meetings and dialogues
  • School and church awareness sessions
  • Distribution of prevention messages

2. Risk Communication

  • Radio broadcasts
  • Community loudspeaker campaigns
  • Social media awareness initiatives
  • Educational materials production

3. Training Community Volunteers

  • Training youth leaders
  • Training community focal points
  • Strengthening local response capacity

4. Protection of Women and Girls

  • Targeted awareness sessions
  • Protection messaging
  • Referral to available support services

5. Community Mobilization

  • Religious leaders engagement
  • Traditional leaders engagement
  • Youth participation initiatives
  • Women's leadership promotion

Communities We Aim to Reach

  • Women and girls
  • Children and adolescents
  • Youth groups
  • Internally displaced persons (IDPs)
  • Community leaders
  • Teachers and students
  • Vulnerable households
Target: More than 50,000 people reached through awareness campaigns, community outreach and radio programs.
